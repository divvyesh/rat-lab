### HYPOTHESIS VALIDATION (Scientific Rigor)

#### Example Hypothesis 1:
**Statement**: "Early adopters prioritize integration capabilities over price"

**Results**:
- **p-value**: 0.018 (< 0.05, statistically significant ✓)
- **Effect Size**: 0.72 (Cohen's d, medium-large effect)
- **Validated**: ✓ YES
- **Interpretation**: "Strong evidence that integration messaging should lead value proposition for early adopter segment. Price is secondary concern."

#### Example Hypothesis 2:
**Statement**: "Enterprise buyers show higher loss aversion than SMB buyers"

**Results**:
- **p-value**: 0.003 (< 0.05, highly significant ✓)
- **Effect Size**: 1.15 (Cohen's d, large effect)
- **Validated**: ✓ YES
- **Interpretation**: "Enterprise segment requires stronger risk mitigation messaging. Emphasize security, compliance, and support guarantees."

#### Example Hypothesis 3:
**Statement**: "Price sensitivity decreases with company size"

**Results**:
- **p-value**: 0.089 (> 0.05, not significant ✗)
- **Effect Size**: 0.31 (Cohen's d, small effect)
- **Validated**: ✗ NO
- **Interpretation**: "Insufficient evidence to support this claim. Price sensitivity appears consistent across segments in this sample."

### REGRESSION ANALYSIS

*Example output:*

> "Multiple regression analysis reveals that cognitive reflection (β = 0.68, p < 0.001) and novelty seeking (β = 0.52, p < 0.01) are the strongest predictors of positive product response. Risk aversion shows negative correlation (β = -0.43, p < 0.05), suggesting that conservative personas require additional trust signals. Price sensitivity did not significantly predict adoption likelihood (β = -0.12, p = 0.23), indicating that value perception outweighs absolute price concerns."

**Translation for investors**: "We know exactly which psychological traits drive adoption. This tells us who to target and how to message them."
